Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about using GL-Chemdraw.

Getting Started

What is GL-Chemdraw?
GL-Chemdraw is a professional chemical structure drawing application built by AIVON for GL Chemtec. It lets you draw molecules, look up compounds from PubChem, view 3D structures, and export in standard chemical formats.
How do I draw a structure?
Use the drawing canvas. Select tools from the left toolbar: bonds, atoms, rings. Click and drag to draw. Use ring templates from the bottom toolbar for common rings like benzene or cyclohexane.
How do I search for a compound by name?
Type the compound name in the search box in the top toolbar and press Enter or click Search. GL-Chemdraw looks up the structure from PubChem and inserts it into your canvas.

Structure & Layout

How do I clean up a messy structure?
Select the structure and press Ctrl+L for Layout (fixes bond lengths and angles) or Ctrl+Shift+L for Clean (standardizes aromatics, etc.). You can also click the Layout button in the Chemical Info panel below the canvas.
How do I draw peptides, RNA, or DNA?
Click the Biopolymer button in the Chemical Info panel, or use Ctrl+Alt+P (Peptide), Ctrl+Alt+R (RNA), or Ctrl+Alt+D (DNA). Enter the sequence and it will be drawn automatically.
How do I draw reaction arrows?
Draw your reactant and product structures. Then select the arrow tool from the left toolbar (reaction/arrow icon). Click and drag between structures to add an arrow. Click the Reactions button in the panel for more details.

Copy, Paste & Export

How do I copy a structure as an image?
Select the structure (or leave nothing selected for the full canvas) and press Ctrl+C. The structure is copied as a PNG image with transparent background. Paste into Word, PowerPoint, or any document.
How do I export to MOL, SDF, or SMILES?
Use the Export menu in the Chemical Info panel (or File menu). Choose MOL, SDF, or SMILES. The file will be downloaded.
How do I paste a structure from the clipboard?
Press Ctrl+V or click the Paste button in the Chemical Info panel. If Ctrl+V doesn't work in the canvas, use the Paste button as a fallback.

PubChem & 3D

How does PubChem lookup work?
When you select a structure on the canvas, GL-Chemdraw matches it against PubChem. If found, the Chemical Info panel shows properties, identifiers, safety data, and more. Use the search bar to look up by name.
How do I view a 3D structure?
When a compound is recognized from PubChem, a "View 3D" option appears in the Chemical Info panel. Click it to open an interactive 3D viewer. Rotate and zoom to explore the molecule.

Selection & Info

What does "select a structure" mean?
Click on a structure in the canvas to select it. The Chemical Info panel then shows data for that structure only. Click on empty canvas to show info for the full canvas.
What is the Align button for?
Align lets you align selected structures (left, right, top, bottom) or arrange R-group labels. Useful for reaction schemes and tidy layouts.
